About

Building with Purpose: From Code to Community

I have spent the last decade at the intersection of logic and impact. While my background is rooted in deep technical development, I’ve always defined myself by what I create, not the languages I use. I am a builder at heart, driven by a simple mission: to design systems that help people move forward—whether that’s through a smoother digital experience, better health, or a more sustainable environment.

Current Ventures: Nutrition & Resilience

Lately, my focus has shifted toward solving problems I am deeply passionate about: health and human performance.

  • Juan Nutrisyon: I am currently developing this platform to bridge the gap in accessible nutritional guidance. It’s more than an app; it’s an effort to empower people with the data and tools they need to lead healthier lives.

  • PainPace: Born from my own journey navigating a hip injury, PainPace is a project dedicated to the running community. It focuses on the technical and psychological side of injury recovery, helping athletes manage their pace and mindset as they work their way back to the trails.

Beyond the Screen

My work is grounded in the real world. Living near the coast in the Philippines, I’m an active member of the DOERs, a volunteer group dedicated to environmental conservation—from beach cleanups to tree planting. I believe that the same discipline required to maintain an ecosystem (or a long-distance running habit) is what makes for great professional work: consistency, patience, and a long-term view.

Though a hip injury has temporarily changed my stride, I remain a runner at heart. That “endurance mindset” defines my professional life—I don’t just launch projects; I sustain them.

The Next Chapter

After 8 years of dedicated partnership with a long-term client, I am looking for my next challenge. I am seeking opportunities that require a builder’s perspective—roles where strategic thinking, technical reliability, and product ownership intersect. Whether in software engineering, product management, or a multi-disciplinary leadership role, I’m ready to build what’s next.
